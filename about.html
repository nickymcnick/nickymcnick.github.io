ABOUT ME

I have always been an artist and an engineer. Since I was a child in Venezuela and then the U.S., my favorite things to do have been drawing and taking things apart to see how they work. In college those passions translated to theater production where I wore many hats as lighting design, puppeteer and puppet maker, and stage manager.

After college I found myself in manufacturing, designing large industrial motors at Electric Machinery Co., Inc in Minneapolis, Minnesota. During the day I built designs to update old machines and created new technologies. At night as a stage manager I built relationships and processes to run a theater production smoothly. This last year one of my plays won an Ivy award for Best Overall Production in theater and my engineering design team doubled the amount of projects completed per quarter.

I moved to Boston, so my wife could go to graduate school, I came to the Startup Institute because I wanted a career where my engineering brain and artistic heart could solve design problems together.
