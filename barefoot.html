"Barefoot" | Lighting Design

“Barefoot” was a collaboration headed by Anna Hanson from the University of Minnesota/Guthrie Theater Actor training program. As a lighting designer I participated in the initial collaboration fully immersing myself into the process. Once a script and locations formed I began to solidify how I wanted to use light to amplify the themes explored by the actors and musicians.I organized and set up all the lighting equipment and constructed the different lighting looks for the major scenes. Through further rehearsal we came up with the timing of the lighting cues.

  • This is a scene of birth. The intense blue represents water. It is tightly focused on the actor simulating a sort of womb she will come out of.

  • The green announces the arrival of other lifeforms into the narrative.

  • This is a snapshot of a volatile scene between these two characters in the desert. The light is harsh and physically warms the small room, heightening the effect of the violent words and actions of the actors.

  • One of the final scenes ends the life cycle with a being of song walking away and taking life with her as the sun sets. The light focuses on her but dims as her song leaves. The long shadows of the actors creates and somber feeling which culminates in total darkness.
