"The House of Bernarda Alba" | Scene Design

This is a scene design for the dramatic play “The House of Bernarda Alba” by Federico Garcia Lorca. It is a dark play that takes place in the spanish countryside during a period of mourning in a house with a stiff and overbearing mother, her five daughters and the family matriarch. The play is dark and explores themes of death, conformity and role of women in a machista spanish society. Through my design I wanted to support the stiff and suffocating environment created by Bernarda Alba, the mother.

  • My research started by looking at the works of spanish artists. Eventually I can across this Picasso sketch. To me it looked like a stage with an arch for an opening and many created by the stairs. I took the basic parabola shape and transformed the otherwise straight edge proscenium arch into one with more interest.

  • The main room is rotated, inclined and pushed forward for the action on stage to push onto the audience. To further this feeling the beams from the house also protrude out of the arch to extend the stage into to the theater house.

  • Scene design sketches of stage and top view plans

  • The colors and textures remain that of a traditional spanish home. The furniture made of dark woods weathered by time contrasting the youth of the daughters and adding a dark background for Lorca’s use of white to signify innocence and purity throughout the play.

  • The severe angles of the walls and the position of the door in the back are perfect for very dramatic lighting as shown here with the model and a flashlight in a dark room.
