Scenic Painting

Here are some examples of scene paintings done both for class and for a production of ”The Vampire!” at the University of Minnesota Centennial Showboat. This shows my acute understanding of color and shape, using scene painting techniques and materials used since the late 1800s and early 1900s.

  • 5 x 5 ft begnnings a painting by blocking out major color washes.

  • Starting to add details with the psychedelic marbling and folds in the curtain.

  • Finished piece with stenciled details on the curtain and flowers in the vase.

  • Finished piece of large vase with flowers. This was done using dry pigment and hide glue as a binder.

  • My painting partner and Scenic Sharge Anne Henly working on a large cave drop for the University of Minnesota Centennial Showboat production "The Vampire!"

  • Close to completed cave drop. The middle section was then cutout and then a net was attached to keep its shape while still being seethrough.
